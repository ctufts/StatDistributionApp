Gaussian Distribution

Overview

The Gaussian distribution, also known as the normal distribution, describes most naturally occurring phenomenon. It has a bell shaped curve which is shifted and scaled by two parameters: the mean and standard deviation.

Parameters

  • mean (μ): The average value in the distribution. It serves as the location parameter.
  • standard deviation (σ): The average distance values from the distribution are from the mean value. This parameter sets the spread or width of the distribution.

Applications and Examples

The normal distribution is used to model natural phenomenon. Examples of such phenomenon are shown below.

  • Frequencies present in White Noise (double check this one, maybe different wording)
